    • Research Experience

    Patter Recognition in Clinical Data, Masters Thesis

    Guide: Prof. Santosh Noronha Dept. of Chemical Engineering, IIT Bombay
    April, 2013 - July, 2014

      Cancer is a disease known to be affected by mutations. These mutations however may not all be significant. Distinguishing driver mutations from passengers is a non-trivial problem. We are trying to explore integrative approaches in significant mutation discovery by integrating analysis at multiple levels. The current methods are either focused on functional pathway analysis or machine learning. We developed a Galaxy based toolbox to run multiple such prediction tools at once, thus removing the need to convert data formats, in a reproducilbe manner. The end results were displayed as a heatmap, to give an insight into those mutations which are predicted to be drivers, by all the tools, thus possibly reducing the set of mutations to the 'real' drivers.
      As part of a collaboration with Dr. Sanjeeva Srivastava's Lab at IIT Bombay, we performed proteomics based microarray analysis of Glioblastoma patients. We use Correspondence Analysis and recursive feature eleimination to preidict a smaller set of markere genes that can be used to differentiate Glioma from normal patients.

      Awarded Undergraduate Research Award(URA03)

      The Galaxy tools are hosted on Testtoolshed here

      The final thesis is available here: http://figshare.com/articles/Pattern_Recognition_in_Clinical_Data/1089638 or here

      Final Presentation: http://figshare.com/articles/Dual_Degree_Project_Presentation/1088457

      A preprint describing the tools is here
